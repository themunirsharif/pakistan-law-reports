Death sentence reduced to life imprisonment where recovered weapon was never forensically matched to crime scene

CourtLahore High Court, Lahore
Case No.Crl. A. Nos. 29270 & 39423 of 2019, Crl. Rev. No. 39425 of 2019, M.R. No. 157 of 2019
Date decided12 December 2023
BenchMalik Shahzad Ahmad Khan and Farooq Haider, JJ.
Criminal & Bail

Headnote

Pakistan Penal Code, 1860 — Ss. 302/324/337-F(iii)/148/149 — Qatl-i-amd — Chance witness — Minor inconsistencies — An injured witness's presence at the time and place of occurrence cannot be doubted, and his testimony carries added evidentiary weight — Minor inconsistencies that appear in witness statements years after the occurrence do not dislodge an otherwise consistent prosecution case — Where a recovered weapon is never forensically compared against crime-scene cartridge cases, the recovery is inconsequential and, combined with a single shot fired, may justify reducing a death sentence to imprisonment for life.

Judgment

Farooq Haider, J. — A single judgment disposed of two criminal appeals, one criminal revision, and a murder reference, all arising from a shooting on 8 October 2014 near a village in Mandi Bahauddin district. Abid Ali and Muhammad Asif were convicted at trial; Abid Ali was sentenced to death under Section 302(b) PPC for the murder of Muhammad Nawaz, and Muhammad Asif received rigorous imprisonment for causing firearm injury to another victim. Fourteen co-accused were acquitted at trial.

The chance-witness objection

Defence counsel argued the prosecution's eyewitnesses were "chance witnesses" who had no residence at the scene, and that their testimony should be discounted on that basis. The Court rejected this: the complainant and his companions had explained a natural, everyday reason for being at that location (returning from checking their agricultural land), and one witness had himself sustained a firearm injury during the occurrence, which placed his presence beyond doubt regardless of residence.

The Court held that minor inconsistencies that surface in witness statements recorded years after the incident do not, by themselves, undermine an otherwise consistent prosecution case, particularly where the broad-daylight nature of the attack and pre-existing familiarity between the parties eliminated any real risk of misidentification.

Medical corroboration

The ocular account was matched against the post-mortem and medico-legal findings: the injuries described by eyewitnesses aligned with the entry wounds and internal damage recorded by the examining doctors, reinforcing rather than contradicting the prosecution's version of events.

Why the sentence was reduced from death to life imprisonment

Although a Kalashnikov rifle was recovered from Abid Ali after his arrest, the forensic agency's report only confirmed the weapon was in working mechanical condition, it never compared the recovered weapon's fired cartridges against the empty cartridge cases collected from the crime scene years earlier, because those cases were never resubmitted for comparison after his arrest. Without that forensic link, the recovery could not meaningfully corroborate the case against him.

Combined with the fact that Abid Ali fired only a single shot, the Court found these two factors sufficient to convert his death sentence to life imprisonment under Section 302(b) PPC, while upholding all other convictions and sentences.

A note to police and forensic authorities

The Court closed by directing that a copy of the judgment be sent to the Inspector General of Police Punjab, the Prosecutor General Punjab, and the Director General of the Punjab Forensic Science Agency, instructing that in future cases involving absconding accused, recovered weapons be properly resubmitted for cartridge-case comparison once the accused is arrested, so that this kind of evidentiary gap does not recur.
